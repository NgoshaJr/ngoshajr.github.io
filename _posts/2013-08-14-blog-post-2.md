Climate change has affected many environments, including the marine and coastal ecosystems. This has led to the loss of biodiversity and an inadequate provision of ecological services. In order to be able to assess and evaluate the impacts of these changes, there must be quick ways to identify changes in environmental behavior. Using the data collected by scientists from buoys and Argo drifters can help provide this information and raise awareness about these changes, as well as enable us to prevent them by monitoring the following parameters: sea surface temperature, dissolved oxygen, salinity, wind speed and direction, e.tc. This may result in the sustainable conservation and management of marine and coastal resources.
